Interactive Question & Answer

What is legacy software?

Legacy software refers to old software that is still performing useful tasks for the community, often developed using earlier programming languages.

What are the attributes of good software?

Good software should be reliable, maintainable, efficient, user-friendly, and scalable.

What is the software process?

The software process describes the activities performed at each stage of a software development project, aiming to develop or evolve software.

What are the generic activities in all software processes?

Generic activities include specification, development, validation, and evolution.

What is the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)?

SDLC is a process used by the software industry to design, develop, and test high-quality software that meets or exceeds customer expectations.

What are the stages of SDLC?

The stages include Planning and Requirement Analysis, Defining Requirements, Designing the Product Architecture, Building the Product, Testing the Product, and Deployment and Maintenance.

What happens during the planning and requirement analysis stage?

This stage involves gathering input from customers, conducting feasibility studies, and planning quality assurance requirements and risk identification.

What is an SRS document?

SRS stands for Software Requirement Specification, which documents all product requirements to be designed and developed during the project life cycle.

What is the purpose of a DDS?

DDS stands for Design Document Specification, which proposes and documents design approaches for the product architecture based on the SRS.

What occurs during the building or developing stage of SDLC?

In this stage, the actual product is built, and programming code is generated according to the DDS while following coding guidelines.

How does testing fit into the SDLC?

Testing is an ongoing activity throughout the SDLC, but this stage specifically involves reporting, tracking, fixing, and retesting product defects until quality standards are met.

What happens during deployment and maintenance?

The product is formally released into the market, possibly starting with a limited segment, followed by user acceptance testing and maintenance based on feedback.

What is the Waterfall Model?

The Waterfall Model is a linear software development process where each phase must be completed before the next begins, emphasizing a sequential design approach.

What is the Iterative Model?

The Iterative Model involves repeating phases multiple times, allowing for refinement and enhancement of the software through successive iterations.

What is the Spiral Model?

The Spiral Model combines iterative development with systematic risk assessment, allowing for gradual refinement through repeated cycles.

What is the V-Model?

The V-Model emphasizes verification and validation, where development and testing activities are planned in parallel, forming a V shape.

What is the Big Bang Model?

The Big Bang Model is an informal approach where development starts without a formal process, often leading to unpredictable results and chaos.

What is Agile Model?

The Agile Model emphasizes flexibility and customer satisfaction through iterative progress and adaptive planning, promoting rapid delivery of functional software.

What is Rapid Application Development (RAD)?

RAD is a software development methodology that focuses on rapid prototyping and quick feedback from users to enhance the product iteratively.

What is Prototyping Model?

The Prototyping Model involves creating early versions of the software to gather user feedback and refine requirements before final development.

What is the importance of requirement analysis?

Requirement analysis is crucial as it sets the foundation for the project by defining what the system should do, ensuring alignment with customer needs.

What is the role of stakeholders in the design phase?

Stakeholders review the design proposals in the DDS and provide input based on risk assessment, budget, and time constraints to select the best approach.

Why is testing an ongoing activity in SDLC?

Testing is integrated throughout the SDLC to identify and resolve defects early, ensuring the final product meets quality standards.

What is the outcome of a successful SDLC?

A successful SDLC results in high-quality software that meets or exceeds customer expectations and is delivered on time and within budget.

What standard defines software life-cycle processes?

ISO/IEC 12207 is an international standard that defines all tasks required for developing and maintaining software.

What is the significance of documentation in SDLC?

Documentation ensures clear communication among stakeholders, provides a reference for requirements and designs, and supports future maintenance efforts.

How does feedback influence the SDLC?

Feedback from users and stakeholders is critical for making iterative improvements, validating requirements, and ensuring the software aligns with user expectations.

What is the focus of the maintenance stage in SDLC?

The maintenance stage focuses on updating the software to fix issues, improve performance, and adapt to changing user needs after initial deployment.

What is the goal of risk assessment in SDLC?

The goal of risk assessment is to identify potential issues that could impact the project and develop strategies to mitigate those risks.

What methodologies are related to software development models?

Related methodologies include Agile Model, RAD Model, Rapid Application Development, and Prototyping Models.
