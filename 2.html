Interactive Question & Answer

What is the Internet?

The Internet is the largest and most well-known computer network, linking millions of computers worldwide.

When was the Internet first developed?

The Internet has operated in one form or another for several decades.

What was ARPANET?

ARPANET was the predecessor of the Internet, created in 1969 and named after the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA), which sponsored its development.

How did ARPANET evolve into the Internet?

It initially connected four supercomputers and eventually evolved into todayâ€™s Internet.

Who proposed the World Wide Web?

Tim Berners-Lee proposed the World Wide Web in 1989.

How did the World Wide Web evolve?

It was originally text-based, but the release of the Mosaic browser in 1993 enabled graphical content.

What is Web 2.0?

Web 2.0 refers to the growing number of web-based applications and services, including social networking, RSS feeds, podcasts, blogs, and wikis.

Who owns the Internet?

The Internet and World Wide Web are not owned by any person, business, or organization.

What is Internet2?

Internet2 is a consortium of researchers, educators, and technology leaders dedicated to the development of revolutionary Internet technologies.

What role do Internet Service Providers (ISPs) play?

ISPs provide access to the Internet, typically for a fee, and include most communication and media companies.

What is the role of infrastructure companies in the Internet?

Infrastructure companies own or operate the physical structure of the Internet, including mobile phone companies, cable companies, and satellite Internet providers.

Is the Internet free?

No, most people and businesses pay for Internet access through ISPs, mobile providers, or leased communication lines.

Does any one entity control the Internet?

No, no single group or organization controls the Internet, though governments can regulate its use within their own countries.

Are the Internet and the World Wide Web the same?

No, the Internet is the physical network, while the World Wide Web is a collection of web pages available over the Internet.

What are the three main decisions when setting up Internet access?

Determining the types of devices, the type of connection, and the Internet Service Provider (ISP) to use.

What devices can connect to the Internet?

Personal computers, smartphones, media tablets, gaming devices, and smart televisions.

What is a direct Internet connection?

A direct connection means the device is continually connected to the Internet, usually via broadband.

What is a dial-up connection?

A dial-up connection uses a modem and telephone line, is inexpensive but slow, and does not support broadband speeds.

What is the most widely used home broadband connection?

Cable Internet, which typically offers speeds around 25 Mbps.

How does DSL work?

DSL (Digital Subscriber Line) delivers broadband over telephone lines but requires a DSL modem and proximity to a switching station.

How does satellite Internet work?

It transmits data using a satellite dish and is an option for rural areas, though it is slower and affected by weather.

What is broadband over fiber (BoF)?

It delivers broadband via fiber-optic cables, also called fiber-to-the-premises (FTTP), offering very fast speeds up to 1 Gbps.

What is a Wi-Fi hotspot?

A Wi-Fi hotspot is a public wireless network, which can be free or fee-based.

What are search sites?

Websites designed to help users search for web pages using search engines and databases, such as Google or Bing.

What is keyword searching?

Typing keywords into a search box to locate relevant web pages, called "hits."

What is phrase searching?

Searching for a phrase by typing multiple keywords together, often enclosed in quotation marks for exact matches.

What are search operators?

Special symbols like quotation marks for exact phrases or a hyphen (-) to exclude words from search results.

What is a field search?

A search limited to a specific web characteristic, such as a page title, URL, or website domain.

How can you evaluate search results?

By considering the title, description, author, source, reliability, and date of publication.

What is instant messaging (IM)?

A service that allows users to exchange real-time messages online.

What is VoIP?

Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) enables making phone calls over the Internet, such as via Skype.

What is a webinar?

A seminar presented via the Web, often with interactive features.

What is social networking?

Platforms that allow users to connect and share information, such as Facebook and Twitter.

What is online shopping?

Buying products or services over the Internet through e-commerce sites.

What is online banking?

Conducting financial transactions over the Internet.

What is online gaming?

Playing games via the Internet, including web-based and multiplayer games.

What is Internet addiction?

The inability to control excessive Internet use, interfering with daily life.

What is Internet censorship?

The restriction of access to certain online content by governments or organizations.

What is Internet filtering?

Using software to block access to certain websites or content.

What are cookies?

Small files stored on a user's device by a web server to track preferences and activities.

What is spyware?

Malicious software that secretly collects user data without their consent.

How can you protect email privacy?

By using encrypted email services and being aware that ISPs and employers can access email messages.
